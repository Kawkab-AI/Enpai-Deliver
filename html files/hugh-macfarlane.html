When our clients get traction in their respective markets, that traction is almost always the result of a good handful of campaigns. Not a silver bullet in sight. Many spinning plates. We might spin up a few new trial campaigns every year, kill a few others throughout the year to maintain the focus on the highest-yield campaigns. Or not. Sometimes we don't make any changes. But we almost never have the luxury of relying on just one or two big swingers. That's one of the (many) differences between B2B and B2C - a crazy good ad campaign for a consumer brand might move the needle for a consumer brand. B2B momentum comes from an aggregate. I don't spend a lot of time in consumer land, but I suspect that's changed in B2C as well. Many plates spinning.
